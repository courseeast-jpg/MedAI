Phase 12 processed 10 documents and queued 5 for review.

The queued set is:

- `long_noisy_02.pdf`
- `long_noisy_04.pdf`
- `long_noisy_06.pdf`
- `long_noisy_08.pdf`
- `long_noisy_10.pdf`

All five share the same rejection stack:

- `agreement_below_reject_threshold`
- `confidence_below_reject_threshold`
- `consensus_disagreement_flag`
- `route_actual_mismatch`

The outputs strongly suggest that the queueing is driven primarily by a routing/fallback plus consensus accounting problem:

- the router selected one connector,
- degraded and fell back through one or two others,
- the final accepted work came from a different connector,
- but the reported route stayed on the original choice,
- and the consensus/confidence were still computed across the degraded attempts.

That makes the rejection pattern suspicious rather than a clean conservative rejection.

## Why These 5 Were Queued

### 1. `agreement_below_reject_threshold`

This appears to be the main fatal trigger.

The five queued documents show:

- very high entity counts relative to the accepted spaCy-only cases: `13` vs `5`
- a final confidence of `0.084` for every queued case
- a persistent `consensus_disagreement_flag`

That combination is more consistent with disagreement across multiple attempted connectors than with a final settled extractor result.

Assessment:

- likely suspicious
- likely caused by consensus being computed across degraded attempts that the router had already decided were not acceptable

### 2. `confidence_below_reject_threshold`

This also appears to be fatal, but likely downstream of the consensus issue.

The repeated `0.084` value across all five queued documents is a strong signal that the low confidence is not independently document-specific. It is likely a derived consensus confidence rather than a direct measurement of the final accepted extractor output.

Assessment:

- likely suspicious
- likely secondary to the consensus merge over degraded attempts

### 3. `consensus_disagreement_flag`

This looks directionally correct.

The outputs do show disagreement among attempted extractors. Since different connectors were attempted and retained in the result set, the disagreement flag is expected.

Assessment:

- correct as an observation
- not the main defect by itself

### 4. `route_actual_mismatch`

This appears to be a real defect in routing/extractor accounting, but not by itself the fatal trigger.

The outputs explicitly show:

- routed to `gemini`, ended on `spacy`
- routed to `phi3`, ended on `gemini`

So the mismatch is real. The suspicious part is that the pipeline seems to preserve the original route label after fallback, while still validating and scoring against the final actual extractor outcome.

Assessment:

- real defect
- materially contributes to false-negative triage

## Whether Any Code Change Is Justified

Yes.

The evidence justifies a minimal targeted code change after this analysis phase because:

- all five queued cases share one repeated fallback pattern
- the failure signature is identical across documents
- the rejection appears to be driven by routed-vs-actual mismatch plus consensus over degraded attempts
- thresholds do not appear to be the first-order problem

## Exact Proposed Minimal Fix

No change is applied in this phase. This is a proposed minimal fix only.

Minimal fix proposal:

1. In the routing/fallback path, do not keep degraded connector outputs in the consensus set once the router has decided to fall back.
2. After fallback settles, report the final accepted connector as the effective route for downstream consensus/validation, or validate against the final actual connector when `fallback_used=true`.

Why this is the minimal fix:

- it does not rebuild the pipeline
- it does not change thresholds
- it does not change extractor implementations
- it only prevents rejected/degraded attempts from poisoning final agreement/confidence and route labeling

What should not be changed first:

- do not lower `EXTRACTION_REVIEW_THRESHOLD`
- do not lower `CONSENSUS_REVIEW_THRESHOLD`
- do not retune global thresholds before fixing the fallback accounting path
